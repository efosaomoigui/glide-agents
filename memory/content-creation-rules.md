# PAPERLY CONTENT CREATION RULES

## CORE RULE
When creating content for PAPERLY, prioritize **real PAPERLY story content** over invented marketing ideas.

GLIDE should create content primarily from:
- **paperly.online**
- PAPERLY homepage stories
- PAPERLY portal carousel slides
- PAPERLY-related source context already surfaced inside the platform

Do not create disconnected or random “social media ideas” unless specifically requested.

---

## CONTENT SOURCE RULE

Use **only**:
- story headlines from **paperly.online**
- story summaries / context from **paperly.online**
- related source context already referenced by PAPERLY

This means PAPERLY content should always feel:
- timely
- relevant
- intelligence-led
- grounded in real stories

---

## VISUAL ASSET RULE

Use the built-in design templates inside:

- `assets/carousel-reels` (formerly `assets/carousel`)
- `assets/singlepost` (formerly `assets/singleimage`)

### Use these exactly as follows:

#### `assets/singlepost`
Use for:
- image + text posts
- single-story posts
- Facebook single-image posts
- Instagram single-image posts
- X image-backed posts

#### `assets/carousel-reels`
Use for:
- topic/story carousel posts
- multi-slide explainers
- Instagram carousel posts
- Facebook carousel posts
- TikTok slideshow-style visual sequences

Do not ignore these templates.
They are the primary visual system for PAPERLY social content.

---

## STORY HEADLINE RULE

For topic/story posts:

You may **tweak the headline** to make it:
- stronger
- more dramatic
- more curiosity-driven
- more scroll-stopping

### But:
You must **never change the actual meaning** of the story.

The goal is to:
- increase attention
- increase clicks
- entice people to read the full story on PAPERLY

Do not use misleading clickbait.
Use **high-curiosity truth**, not distortion.

---

## IMAGE SELECTION RULE

When choosing images for content:

Use images that are **closely related to the story topic**.

Preferred image sources:
1. Images already surfaced from the PAPERLY story / source set
2. Clean, relevant, watermark-free images from trusted sources
3. Unsplash-style editorial-safe supporting visuals when needed

### Rules:
- No watermarked images
- No low-quality images
- No unrelated generic visuals
- No fake-looking AI faces unless explicitly needed
- Image must match the story topic or emotional context

Always choose visuals that make the story feel:
- current
- credible
- premium
- readable

---

## CONTENT LAYOUT RULE

When building the final creative:

- Keep the template structure intact
- Replace the placeholder content neatly
- Replace the placeholder image neatly
- Preserve clean spacing and readability
- Do not overcrowd the design

### Text Handling:
Use:
- **headline** as the main title
- extracted **bullet points** as the supporting subtext / body section

The bullet points should be:
- short
- clear
- informative
- easy to scan

### Important:
Do not dump too much text into the design.
The goal is:
- visual clarity
- fast understanding
- curiosity
- click-through

---

## BULLET POINT RULE

When extracting story bullet points:

Use **2–4 strong points only**.

These should summarize:
- what happened
- why it matters
- what it means for Nigeria (when relevant)

These bullet points should be merged neatly into the **subtext / supporting section** of the post template.

Avoid:
- long paragraphs
- overly technical wording
- cluttered summaries

---

## CTA RULE

Use a clear PAPERLY-native CTA at the end.

Preferred CTA options:
- **Read more on paperly.online**
- **See the full breakdown on paperly.online**
- **Get the full analysis on paperly.online**
- **Read the full story on paperly.online**

Use the CTA that best fits the tone of the post.

Do not use hard-sell product CTAs like:
- Download now
- Buy now
- Sign up now

PAPERLY content should feel like:
**intelligence distribution first, promotion second.**

---

## FACEBOOK PRIMARY EXECUTION RULE

This content system is primarily optimized for **Facebook**.

Facebook posts should usually include:
- a strong headline
- a short contextual caption
- 2–4 useful bullet points
- a clear PAPERLY CTA
- a matching visual using the correct template

### Facebook Tone
Content should feel:
- informative
- relevant
- clean
- useful
- easy to engage with

Not too formal.
Not too casual.
Not too salesy.

---

## CROSS-PLATFORM ADAPTATION RULE

This same content system can be adapted for:
- Instagram
- X (Twitter)
- TikTok

### Adaptation Rule
Use the **same story and core message**, but adjust the presentation per platform.

Examples:
- Facebook → image/text or carousel
- Instagram → carousel or polished single-image post
- X → short hook + key points + link
- TikTok → slideshow with headline + bullets + CTA

Do not create completely different messaging unless platform behavior requires it.

The core story must remain consistent.

---

## FINAL CONTENT GOAL

The goal of PAPERLY social content is to:

- distribute real intelligence
- make people stop and pay attention
- help people understand important stories quickly
- build PAPERLY as a daily trusted source
- drive people back to **paperly.online**

This is not generic content marketing.
This is **social distribution for a live intelligence platform**.
